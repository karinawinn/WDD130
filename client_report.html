Team Project Report

Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I've wanted to create some kind of online portfolio for my physics and programming projects for a long time. With an idea like this, most of the images and text are going to be pulled from previous projects I've done. All I had to do was grab an image from each project I wanted to showcase and write short descriptions and explanations for each project. The images for the home page and the logo were a bit harder to come up with on my own, so I had Gemini generate some images that represented the scientific and programming principles that I wanted to show.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

First we communicated via canvas messages. Then we used Teams. It took me some time to find the channel William (lead developer) had created, but once I did, there were no communication issues.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?

Yes, the development team met all my expectations for my site. All of the content I sent was included and the pages were laid out in accordance with my wireframes and used the colors and fonts I suggested. Overall, the pages look great and I'm very happy with them.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?

I think the only thing that I would do differently is check up more often with my development team. In the second week especially, I didn't reach out or check in very often and wasn't very well informed of how the project was going.

Lead Developer Report

How did you manage your development team and comunicate the tasks for your junior devs?

Our first messages were sent via canvas, but later we switched to Microsoft Teams. By the end of the first week I got the site plan from the client and assigned pages to both junior developers.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

Our first communications were over canvas messages, then we talked over email for a bit. Then we ultimately switched to Microsoft Teams with the rest of the team. There weren't any issues with communication.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?

During the first week, I looked up some instructions for how to publish the site on GitHub. I was able to figure it out pretty easily and got it published. Than after we had completed the HTML, I sent the link to my client and asked her to send feedback if she had any. I did the same thing once we had completed and published the CSS.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

It was pretty good overall. I hit a bit of a rough patch in the start with some issues managing branches, but by the end of the first week I was able to merge the branches and there weren't any problems after that.

Junior Developer Report

How did you communicate with your team lead? Was there any issues?

We sent messages on Microsoft Teams. It did take him a while to answer messages sometimes (he was pretty busy), but there weren't problems other than that.

Did you know what was expected of you for each site?

Yes. During the second week I recieved wireframes for each of the pages I was assigned along with images and text content.

Were you able to contribute to your sites?

Yes. Within the first week I was added as a contributor to each repository and was able to push my code to it and make changes when needed.

Did you run into any issues with GitHub and adding your changes to the sites?

No, I didn't have any issues pushing changes to either repository. I learned from my mishap as a Lead Developer and pulled each time before writing or editing code.

General Experiences and Reflections

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The hardest part of this project for me was being in the lead developer role. There were definitely more responsibilities than those of the junior developer role, and I felt it. I was able to overcome this by dedicating more time to developing the site and making sure I communicated well with my junior developers.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

I really enjoyed writing the css for the project I was lead developer for. It was challenging, but it helped me learn new things. The client's vision for the page was really awesome and I had a lot of fun making it happen.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

If I could do this again, I would start my tasks earlier in the week. I ususally wouln't start coding until Thursay or Friday, which left some but not a lot of time for my junior developers to do their tasks. Starting on Monday or Tuesday would have fixed that problem.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

Yes I do think I am more qualified to work on real-world projects. The media queries and adjustable sizes I adeed worked really well and made the pages easy to view on smaller screens and mobile devices. I'm really proud of the pages that I created.
